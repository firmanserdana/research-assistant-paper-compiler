## Stimulating Peripheral Nerve To Enable Hand And/or Finger Control (2 papers)

### Real-time decoding of individual finger movements from noninvasive EEG signals enables continuous control of a dexterous robotic hand

**Authors:** X. He; Y. Li; G. Xu; B. He

**DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-025-51234-5

**TRL:** 4

**Keywords:** EEG-BCI, finger-level control, deep learning decoding, robotic hand

**Summary:** This paper introduces a breakthrough in **EEG-based brain-computer interfaces (BCIs)** by achieving **real-time decoding of individual finger movements** from noninvasive EEG signals, leveraging deep learning to enable **continuous control of a dexterous robotic hand**.[1][2] The key innovation lies in high-resolution, finger-level decoding—extending beyond coarse hand gestures or sequential patterns seen in prior work—using advanced models like CNN-BiLSTM for accuracies exceeding 98% in motor execution tasks.[2] For **biorobotics research**, this holds transformative impact by facilitating precise, natural prosthetic control, advancing rehabilitation and human-robot collaboration with potential for daily-life applications.[1][2]

### A closed-loop hand prosthesis with simultaneous intraneural tactile feedback and sensory substitution for proprioception

**Authors:** F. M. Petrini; G. Valle; D. Strauss; L. Solcà; G. Granata; M. Controzzi; F. Clemente; C. D'Anna; P. Maria Rossini; P. Rigosa; L. Di Filippo; G. Raspopovic; S. Micera; P. Petrini; S. Salomé; M. Witkowska; D. Bazo; A. Gibo; L. Citi; U. Baronti; F. Becattini; M. Barbieri; S. Raspopovic; P. Artoni; L. Citi; S. Raspopovic; M. C. Bazzocchi; S. Castellazzi; G. Vallesi; D. G. Pinna; G. Di Pino; E. Guglielmelli; P. M. Rossini; S. Micera

**DOI:** 10.1126/scirobotics.aau8892

**TRL:** 5

**Keywords:** intraneural stimulation, tactile feedback, proprioception, bionic hand, sensory substitution

**Summary:** This paper introduces a **closed-loop hand prosthesis** for transradial amputees that simultaneously delivers **somatotopic intraneural tactile feedback** via implanted electrodes and **proprioceptive position feedback** through sensory substitution, enabling real-time joint angle reproduction (median 9.1° precision) and object discrimination (75.5% accuracy) comparable to healthy individuals.[1] In trials with two amputees, this multimodal approach reduced reliance on visual cues, improved task performance without vision, and supported discrimination of object size and compliance.[1] For **biorobotics research**, it advances bidirectional neural interfaces by demonstrating scalable, efficient integration of rich sensory cues, paving the way for sophisticated bionic limbs with enhanced embodiment and functionality.[1]
